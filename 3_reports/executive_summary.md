# Executive Summary

This project analyzes customer retention and customer value using the Online Retail II dataset through a combination of Cohort Retention Analysis and RFM Customer Segmentation.

The objective was to understand how effectively the business retains customers after acquisition, identify the most valuable customer groups, and uncover opportunities to improve long-term customer lifetime value.

## Key Findings

* The business analyzed **5,878 unique customers** generating **£17.37M in revenue**.
* Customer retention declines sharply after the initial purchase, with **Month 2 retention averaging only 23%**, indicating significant early-stage customer churn.
* The strongest-performing cohort (**December 2009**) achieved **35% Month 2 retention**, substantially outperforming other customer groups.
* Revenue is heavily concentrated among a relatively small group of highly engaged customers identified through RFM segmentation.
* The **At Risk** segment represents the largest customer group, indicating substantial revenue exposure if re-engagement efforts are not implemented.

## Business Implications

The analysis suggests that the business is effective at acquiring customers but struggles to convert first-time buyers into repeat customers. The largest opportunity for growth is improving retention immediately after the first purchase.

RFM segmentation further reveals that customer value is unevenly distributed, with Champions and Loyal Customers contributing a disproportionate share of revenue. Protecting these customers while reactivating At Risk customers should be prioritized to maximize customer lifetime value.

## Recommendations

* Implement automated post-purchase campaigns targeting customers within 14–30 days of their first order.
* Develop segment-specific marketing strategies using RFM classifications rather than broad customer-wide promotions.
* Introduce loyalty and retention initiatives focused on high-value customer segments.
* Launch reactivation campaigns targeting At Risk customers before they transition into Lost customers.

By combining retention analytics with customer segmentation, this project demonstrates how transactional data can be transformed into actionable insights that support customer growth, retention strategy, and long-term revenue optimization.
